ABOUT

Who We Are

    We are a trainee founded and led mentorship initiative that serves students from backgrounds underrepresented in academia, including (but not limited to) students of color, first generation students, low-income students, and students identifying as LGBTQ+, who are interested in applying to PhD programs in the mind sciences.  Students from underrepresented backgrounds often lack access to mentorship that bolsters more privileged students while applying to graduate programs. To contribute to eliminating these inequities, we organize a  yearly weekend workshop intensive that is focused on demystifying the ins and outs of graduate school applications. We also pair students with one-on-one mentors that offer ongoing support for the students throughout the application process and into their academic careers.

  We believe our goal to support students from underrepresented backgrounds is necessary, not only for equity, but for the vitality and relevance of the mind sciences.

ABOUT

Our Participants

Our first year we received 100 applications from candidates across the United States. Our inaugural class of 21 participants have faced unique barriers in their academic paths and had exciting interdisciplinary interests in a variety of mind science fields. While we are unable to convey the full richness of their backgrounds and interests,  below we share several figures summarizing some demographic facets of DivE In 2021 participants. 

Composite Scores

DivE In is committed to serving students from backgrounds underrepresented in academia who would receive the most benefit from additional mentorship. To reflect this goal, our admissions decisions were made based on a composite score that considered need, fit, and merit. 

Race

Majority of our participants identified as Black, Indigenous, or Persons of Color. 

LGBTQ+

DivE In defines "underrepresented background" broadly, including persons identifying as LGBTQ+, first generation college students, and students with disability. 38% of DivE In 2021 identified as LGBTQ+.

Access to mentorship

Over 40% of our participants reported that before the program they didn't know any person in the field whom they could ask questions.

Participant satisfaction

Overall, participants were extremely satisfied with the mind DivE In weekend (M = 8.83, SD = 0.79, where 0 = Not at all satisfied and 10 = Completely satisfied). 100% of participants agreed that mind DivE In was worth their time, with the majority of participants reporting that the weekend exceeded their expectations.

Workshop outcomes

Comparing the post-workshop survey to the questions asked during the admissions process revealed that attendees felt significantly more confident and knowledgeable about the graduate school admissions process, and more comfortable reaching out for mentorship after the workshop.
